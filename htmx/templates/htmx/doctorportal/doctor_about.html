What is the PPST?

The Philadelphia Pointing Span Test (PPST) is a cognitive assessment tool designed to measure attention, working memory, and processing speed. It evaluates an individual's capacity to track and recall sequentially presented visual stimuli, providing valuable insights for clinical and research purposes.

Purpose & Benefits

Healthcare professionals use the PPST to:

  • Assess cognitive function: Measure attention span and working memory capacity
  • Diagnose conditions: Identify potential cognitive impairments or neurodevelopmental disorders
  • Monitor progress: Track changes in cognitive performance over time for treatment planning
  • Support research: Contribute to cognitive science and neuropsychological research studies

How the PPST Works

The test presents a series of stimuli (digits and/or mixed alphanumeric symbols) to participants, who must respond accurately to maintain performance accuracy. The assessment measures:

  • Response accuracy and consistency
  • Processing speed and reaction time
  • Sustained attention across multiple trials
  • Working memory capacity

Platform Features

This PPST platform includes bilingual testing, accessibility color themes, randomized digit and mixed stimuli, doctor-created test links, latency tracking, result dashboards, CSV export, and patient comparison by age group.

Getting Started

To administer the PPST to your patients or research participants:

  • Create a new test using the "Create Test" feature in your dashboard
  • Configure test parameters and generate a participant link
  • Share the link with your participant
  • Access results and analysis once the test is complete
